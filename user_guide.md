# Galen Phonation Interface - User Guide

Welcome to the **Galen Phonation Interface**, an interactive digital tool designed to explore Galen's ancient physiological theories of voice production. This guide will help you navigate the interface and make the most of its features.

## 1. Interface Overview

The interface is divided into three main areas:

*   **Navigation Bar (Left)**: A sidebar with icons to switch between different modes of exploration.
*   **Media Viewer (Center)**: The main stage where visual content (images, videos, animations) is displayed.
*   **Narrative Panel (Right)**: The text area containing descriptions, historical context, and analysis.

## 2. Navigation

You can switch between four main sections using the icons on the left sidebar:

*   **🏠 Home (Intro)**: Returns you to the landing page with the general introduction.
*   **🛡️ Anatomy (Larynx)**: Focuses on the physical structure of the larynx (cartilages, muscles, nerves).
*   **🌬️ Physiology (Pneuma)**: Explores the movement of *pneuma* (spirit/breath) and the generation of voice.
*   **📜 Textual Analysis**: profound look into the original Greek texts and their translations.

## 3. How to Use the Interface

### Interactive Text & Glossary
As you read through the **Narrative Panel**, you will notice certain terms highlighted in **red**.
*   **Clicking a term** opens a **Glossary Card** with a definition, historical context, and a link to the full entry on the [ATLOMY](https://www.atlomy.com) website.
*   To close the glossary card, simply click the **X** or anywhere outside the card.

### Visualizations
The **Media Viewer** automatically updates based on the text you are reading.
*   **Images & diagrams**: Static anatomical illustrations.
*   **Videos**: Detailed 3D animations of the glottis and laryngeal movement.
*   **Pneuma Visualizer**: In the *Physiology* section, you will see a schematic animation showing the flow of *pneuma* from the brain, through the nerves, to the muscles and larynx. This animation evolves step-by-step as you scroll or click through the narrative.

### Textual Analysis
In the **Analysis** section, the interface displays:
*   The **English translation** of Galen's text.
*   The **Original Greek text** for comparison.
*   Relevant visual references from the ATLOMY project.

## 4. Tips & Tricks

*   **Sharing**: The URL (web address) automatically updates as you navigate. You can copy and share the link to send someone to a specific section and step (e.g., `?section=pneuma&step=3`).
*   **Mobile Support**: The interface is responsive. On mobile devices, the narrative panel appears below the media viewer.
